Theory and Design

Why the system is split into a biological embedding layer and a compressed retrieval layer.

This page explains the project's theory, design tradeoffs, and architecture so the package reads as a system, not just a set of functions.

Problem framing

Single-cell atlas retrieval asks for both semantic fidelity and operational efficiency. TurboCell Atlas treats those as different layers. A strong upstream embedding model captures biology. TurboQuant makes candidate search lighter. Exact reranking protects the final ranking.

Why exact reranking stays in the design

Nearest-neighbor differences in atlas space can change interpretation, enrichment results, and which cell states appear most relevant. For that reason, approximation is allowed for candidate generation, but not trusted as the final ranking by default.

Architecture

tca.config validates search settings. tca.quantization owns TurboQuant encoding. tca.pipeline owns filtering, candidate generation, and reranking. tca.io handles local inputs. tca.benchmarks keeps alternative search baselines isolated from the core.
